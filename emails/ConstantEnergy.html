The State of Skip-a-Pay Today

Most credit unions allow members to apply online, but the application is still processed manually. Imagine filling your cart on Amazon, then being asked to wait to pay. Members expect to resolve their requests online.

Here's the problem:

  • FIs are adding online forms to make it easier for members to apply.
  • Online forms increase back office work (new channel) since manual processing still occurs.
  • Members have to wait hours or days for an answer ‑ approved or declined.

With Zero Processing Skip‑a‑Pay, credit unions can move away from online forms, phone calls and branch visits that inundate the back office.
